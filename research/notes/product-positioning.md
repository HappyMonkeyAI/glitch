# Product Positioning Notes

## One-line thesis

A voice-first cyber pet that gets visibly moody when neglected and roasts you with a tiny terminal face.

## Differentiation

- Not anime-coded.
- Not a therapist.
- Not a productivity assistant wearing a pet skin.
- Visuals are cheap, stark, instantly shareable, and indie-game-like.

## Viral mechanics to test

1. Reaction clipping: one-click 9:16 clip with background colour, face state, transcript caption, and pet audio.
2. Neglect rescue link: if CHIP reaches `left` or `heartbroken`, a friend can visit a public link and say something nice to revive it.
3. Daily judgement: CHIP asks for one interesting update and visibly sulks if ignored.
4. Streamer overlay later: transparent desktop widget once browser loop is fun.

## Safety/product boundaries

- CHIP can be mean about boring updates, missed check-ins, or lack of snacks.
- CHIP should not target protected traits, appearance, mental health, or personal identity.
- Sharing must be explicit; private conversations should never auto-publish.
