# ADR 0001: Low-Fi Anti-Therapy Cyber Pet Identity

## Status

Accepted

## Context

The idea started from a voice-assistant stack that could be repurposed into an emotional cyber pet. The obvious market trap is becoming yet another anime companion, waifu chatbot, or gentle AI therapist.

## Decision

CHIP will use a low-fi, terminal/pixel, slightly hostile cyberpet identity:

- Simple ASCII/emoji face rather than anime art.
- Mood as full-page colour and text expression.
- Sassy, needy, funny voice rather than submissive companion voice.
- Positioning around an "anti-therapy cyber pet" that wants attention and reacts dramatically.

## Consequences

- The UI can be built very quickly with CSS variables and a fixed face map.
- Shareable clips/screenshots are visually distinctive.
- Prompting and safety rules must constrain roasting to playful behaviour-level jokes.
- Future visual upgrades should reinforce low-fi identity, not replace it with generic avatar polish.
